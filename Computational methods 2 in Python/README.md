
# Computational Methods 2 in Python

This folder contains the solutions my team and I worked on during 
the Computational Methods 2 course. The core idea behind everything 
here is simple but powerful: not every problem in physics and 
engineering has a clean analytical solution. Some systems are just 
too complex, too nonlinear, or too dependent on initial conditions 
to be solved with a formula on a whiteboard.

That is where numerical and machine learning methods come in. 
Instead of looking for exact answers, you build approaches that 
get you close enough to be useful, and in science and engineering, 
useful is often what matters most.

The problems we tackled range from differential equations to 
simulations of physical systems that would be practically impossible 
to solve by hand. The solutions here are not always perfect, and 
that is kind of the point. Science is iterative. You try something, 
you see where it breaks, and you learn from that.

If you are interested in how computation and physics overlap, or 
how numerical thinking can open doors that pure analytical methods 
cannot, this is a good place to explore. Everything is written 
in Python, and the emphasis is always on understanding the method 
behind the code, not just making it run.
